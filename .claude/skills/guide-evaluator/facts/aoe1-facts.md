# AoE1 Facts Reference (ground truth for fact-checking guides)

**Scope**: ORIGINAL Age of Empires (1997), including the Rise of Rome expansion (1998) mechanics/units where noted. This file does NOT cover Age of Empires: Definitive Edition (2018 rebalance, sometimes called "1a" by the community) or "Return of Rome" (the AoE2:DE game mode). Where original-game numbers differ from DE, the ORIGINAL value is what's recorded here.

**Reviewed by**: domain expert (user), 2026-07-06 - core facts confirmed: "cung A" = Composite Bowman (foot archer), "cung R" = Chariot Archer (mounted archer) - two DIFFERENT units; neither hard-counters any class and Composite Bowman has no armor negation; Slinger ("quẩy đá") gets no bonus vs mounted archers; build conventions ("7 thực 3 vàng", "23 dân đời 3 không thành") correct. Minor patch/stat numbers still marked ⚠️ below.

**Vocabulary map** (Vietnamese community slang → game unit):
- "cung A" = the foot-archer line: Bowman → Improved Bowman → Composite Bowman
- "ngựa chém"/"ngựa" = melee cavalry line: Scout → Cavalry → Heavy Cavalry → Cataphract
- "ngựa dò" = Scout (the starting exploration cavalry unit)
- "lạc đà" = Camel Rider (Rise of Rome only, Bronze Age, trained at Stable)
- "kỵ cung" = Horse Archer / Heavy Horse Archer, or more broadly any mounted archer (Chariot Archer, Elephant Archer are NOT cavalry-archer stat-wise but fill a similar "hit and run" niche)
- "cung A" = Composite Bowman - a FOOT archer (confirmed by domain expert). NO armor negation, NO bonus-damage-vs-a-unit-class. Edge = cost + range only.
- "cung R" = Chariot Archer - a MOUNTED archer / kỵ cung (confirmed by domain expert). Edge = mobility / hit-and-run. Like cung A, it does NOT hard-counter any unit class. The guide `cung-r-ngua-chem-aoe4` uses "cung R" = Chariot Archer CORRECTLY (no naming error). The factual error in that guide is only the implication that "cung R khắc tất" (hard-counters everything) - FALSE.

## Foot archers / Cung A

- Bowman (Tool Age): 35 HP, 3 attack, 5 range, cost 40 food + 20 wood. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html
- Improved Bowman (Bronze Age): 40 HP, 4 attack, 6 range, cost 40 food + 20 gold. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html
- Composite Bowman (Bronze Age): 45 HP, 5 attack, 7 range, cost 40 food + 20 gold, 0 base armor. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html, https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/composite-bowman/
- Composite Bowman is notably cheaper (~half the resources) than the Chariot Archer, making it one of the most cost-effective Bronze Age ranged units when massed. - source: WebSearch summary of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Composite_Bowman_(Age_of_Empires)
- Archers as a whole are described as requiring infantry cover - they die quickly if caught in melee without protection. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html
- **Cung R has NO hard-counter/bonus-damage vs any specific unit type.** Its strength is cost-effectiveness, range, and (for mounted variants) mobility for hit-and-run - not an attack bonus against a unit class the way Camel Rider has bonus attack vs cavalry, or Slinger has bonus attack vs archers. This is the key correction: a claim that "cung R hard-counters everything" is FALSE for the original game. It applies to BOTH cung A (Composite Bowman, foot) AND cung R (Chariot Archer, mounted) - neither hard-counters a unit class. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html + domain-expert confirmation (2026-07-06). NOTE: the armor-ignoring/pierce mechanic belongs to AoE2's Composite Bowman ONLY; the original AoE1 cung A does NOT ignore armor.
- Archers (Composite Bowman explicitly) "die miserably against Stone Throwers/Catapults" - siege is a hard counter to foot archers. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/composite-bowman/
- Slinger (Rise of Rome, Barracks, Tool Age) has a +3 attack bonus specifically against archers, plus 3 pierce armor - a dedicated anti-archer unit. - source: WebSearch of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Slinger_(Age_of_Empires) and https://liquipedia.net/ageofempires/Slinger
- Slinger ("quẩy đá") anti-archer bonus applies ONLY to foot archers, NOT to mounted/cavalry archers (Chariot Archer, Horse Archer, Elephant Archer). - CONFIRMED by domain expert (2026-07-06).
- Priests are also a listed threat to "protected Archers" per artho.com's archer strategy note, implying massed conversion can also strip away archer-heavy armies. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html

## Cavalry / Ngựa chém

- Scout (starting unit, weakest cavalry line member, cannot be upgraded): 60 HP, 3 attack. Used for map exploration, finding sheep/forage, and scouting the enemy - not a combat unit. - source: WebSearch of https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/scout/
- Cavalry (Bronze Age): 150 HP, 8 attack, 0 armor, cost 70 food + 80 gold. Has a cavalry charge bonus of +5 attack against infantry. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/cavalry/
- Cavalry's main hard counter is the Hoplite line ("great Cavalry killers"). - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/cavalry/
- Heavy Cavalry (Iron Age, upgrade of Cavalry): 150 HP, 10 attack, 1 armor, 1 pierce armor, cost 70 food + 80 gold. Keeps the +5 vs infantry charge bonus, and gains +1 pierce armor specifically vs Ballista/Helepolis/missile weapons. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/heavy-cavalry/
- Heavy Cavalry is still weak to Iron Age archers, which by that age have more range, armor, and attack. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/heavy-cavalry/
- Cataphract (Rise of Rome, Iron Age upgrade of Heavy Cavalry, Byzantine-line unique-ish top upgrade): 180 HP, 12 attack, 3 armor, 1 pierce armor, +5 vs infantry charge bonus, +7 vs Light Infantry. - source: WebSearch aggregation citing https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Cataphract_(Age_of_Empires) and https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/cataphract/
- Cataphract still has cavalry-level (low) pierce armor, so massed archers remain a valid counter even against the fully upgraded unit. ⚠️ CẦN NGƯỜI DUYỆT (this specific framing came from an AoE2 Cataphract discussion thread, not a primary AoE1 source - the underlying pierce-armor-weakness logic likely carries over but needs a dedicated AoE1 source check)
- "Ngựa chém sợ lạc đà" (melee cavalry fears the camel) is TRUE for the original game via the Camel Rider's dedicated anti-cavalry bonus (see Camel section below) - this is a real, sourced hard counter, unlike the archer case.

## Camels / Lạc đà (Rise of Rome only)

- Camel Rider: Bronze Age, trained at the Stable, requires Town Center + Barracks + Stable. Cost 70 food + 60 gold, 125 HP, base attack 6, 0 armor, fast movement. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/camel-rider/
- Camel Rider gets +8 attack vs cavalry/horse units and +4 attack vs chariot units - this is the real, sourced "camel hard-counters horse" mechanic the guides reference. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/camel-rider/, corroborated by https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Camel_Rider_(Age_of_Empires)
- Camel Rider cannot be upgraded past the Bronze-Age version, so it loses relative effectiveness in the Iron Age; a fully upgraded Cataphract can eventually beat it. - source: WebSearch summary of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Camel_Rider_(Age_of_Empires)
- Camel Rider otherwise has weak base attack/armor and is a niche, situational unit outside of its anti-cavalry role - it does poorly against infantry. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/camel-rider/
- Palmyrans (a Rise of Rome civilization) have a unique camel speed bonus (~+25%) that lets their camels catch fast Heavy Horse Archers - this matches the guide's "lạc đà bay Palmyran" reference. - source: WebSearch summary of Horse Archer heavengames page and Camel Rider heavengames page
- Camel Rider does not counter infantry - melee foot units beat it. - source: https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/camel-rider/ ⚠️ CẦN NGƯỜI DUYỆT for the "20%" or similar specific fear/damage-debuff numbers if a guide states an exact percentage - no source found for a "-X% damage to nearby enemy horses" aura effect in the original game; that mechanic (auras reducing enemy damage near a unit) sounds more like an AoE4 Abbasid Camel Archer/Rider mechanic and should NOT be attributed to original AoE1.

## Kỵ cung / mounted archers (Chariot Archer = "cung R", Horse Archer, Elephant Archer)

- Chariot Archer = "cung R" (the guide's central unit): a mounted archer, classed as a chariot unit. Strength = mobility / hit-and-run, NOT a hard-counter against any unit class. Countered by Camel Rider (which has +4 attack vs chariots) and by faster/anti-archer units. Civs like Hittites ("Hit") and Assyrians ("Assy") favor it for the classic hit-and-run style, as the guide states. - source: domain-expert confirmation (2026-07-06) + Camel Rider bonus-vs-chariot fact below.
- Horse Archer: fast, strong attack and range; has an armor bonus specifically against missile/archer units (other ranged units). Upgrades to Heavy Horse Archer. - source: WebSearch summary of https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/horse-archer/
- In equal-number fights, Horse Archers are described as beating Chariot Archers, Elephant Archers, and Composite Bowmen. - source: WebSearch summary of Horse Archer heavengames/forum discussion
- Horse Archer's main dedicated counter is the Camel Rider (Rise of Rome), especially Palmyran camels which get enough speed to catch Heavy Horse Archers. - source: same as above
- Priests are vulnerable to fast Horse Archers, which can kill a Priest before a conversion completes; Chariot/Chariot Archer units resist conversion better and are the "safest" way to snipe a Priest. - source: WebSearch summary of https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/multiplayerstrategies/priest-teaming/ and related forum threads

## Economy & Build Order

- The 7-food-villager + 3-gold-villager opening ("build 7 thực 3 vàng") is a CORRECT, established community build-order convention for original AoE. - CONFIRMED by domain expert (2026-07-06). It is a convention (not an engine rule) but accurate as stated.
- "23 dân, đời 3 không thành" (advance to Age 3 around 23 population, plus the competitive "no towers/castle" house-rule) is a CORRECT, accepted convention in the Vietnamese AoE1 scene. - CONFIRMED by domain expert (2026-07-06). It is a competitive house-rule, not engine-enforced, but accurate as community practice.
- Scout is the standard unit to find sheep/forage and scout the map at the start of a game (matches "ngựa dò" usage in the guides). - source: WebSearch summary of https://aoe.heavengames.com/theacademy/unitsboatsandbuildings/scout/
- Town Center never idling and constant villager production are universal AoE-series economy fundamentals; no reason to doubt these apply to the original game the same as later titles, but no original-AoE-specific primary source was pulled for this exact phrasing. ⚠️ CẦN NGƯỜI DUYỆT (general knowledge, not directly sourced in this research pass)

## Counters & Combat Model (general)

- The original AoE combat model is NOT a strict rock-paper-scissors triangle the way AoE2/AoE4 communities describe it (e.g., AoE4's "spear beats cavalry, cavalry beats archer, archer beats spear" cycle). Original AoE counters are a looser set of specific unit-vs-unit attack bonuses (Camel vs cavalry, Slinger vs archer, Hoplite vs cavalry) rather than one universal triangle covering all unit classes. - source: synthesis of unit-specific findings above; general combat-model overview page (https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Counter) could not be fetched directly (HTTP 402), so this synthesis is inferred from individual unit pages, not a single authoritative counters-overview page. ⚠️ CẦN NGƯỜI DUYỆT
- Stone Thrower / Catapult (siege) are the hard, generalized counter to massed foot archers: siege attacks inflict melee (not pierce) damage and hit hard against low-HP ranged units; siege in turn is vulnerable to cavalry/melee units that can close the distance, since siege can't fight at close range. - source: WebSearch summary of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult_(Age_of_Empires) and https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Thrower
- Infantry (upgraded foot soldiers) are also noted as strong specifically against Villagers, Archers, and Catapults. - source: WebSearch summary of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Unit_(Age_of_Empires)

## Siege

- Stone Thrower is the first, and in Bronze Age the only, siege unit buildable at the Siege Workshop. - source: WebSearch summary of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Stone_Thrower
- Stone Throwers are vulnerable to fast hit-and-run units (e.g., Horse Archers) that can dart in, fire, and retreat before the slow-firing Stone Thrower can respond. - source: WebSearch summary of forum/wiki discussion of Stone Thrower/Catapult
- Catapult (upgrade of Stone Thrower) adds roughly +10 damage, +2 range, +1 pierce armor, +0.45 blast radius over the Stone Thrower; Heavy Catapult (further upgrade) adds +75 HP, +1 range, +1 pierce armor, +20% blast radius, and can clear tree lines. - source: WebSearch summary of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Catapult_(Age_of_Empires) ⚠️ CẦN NGƯỜI DUYỆT (numbers came from an aggregated search summary, not a directly verified stat table - worth double-checking exact figures)
- Siege units cannot fight effectively at close range and are vulnerable especially to cavalry charges. - source: WebSearch summary of Catapult wiki page

## Priests

- Priest (Bronze Age): 25 HP, 10 range, cost 125 gold. Cannot attack directly; heals friendly units and converts enemy units to your side. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html
- Priests are vulnerable to Chariots, Catapults/Stone Throwers, and protected/escorted Archers - a single well-aimed Stone Thrower hit is lethal and forces the Priest to choose between finishing a conversion or dodging and dying. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html, WebSearch summary of priest-teaming/forum threads
- Chariots and Chariot Archers have higher resistance to conversion than other unit types, making them relatively safe to use for hunting down enemy Priests. - source: WebSearch summary of https://ageofempires.fandom.com/wiki/Conversion
- Slow units like War Elephants and Elephant Archers are especially prone to being converted (long exposure time to a Priest). - source: WebSearch summary of Conversion-related forum/wiki threads
- Researching Monotheism enables converting/capturing entire enemy structures (not just units) without destroying them first. - source: https://artho.com/age/units1.html
